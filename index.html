SafeVoice — Open source community safety

About These Reports

All reports on SafeVoice are submitted anonymously by community members and are completely unverified. We publish them because we believe people have the right to share their experiences and warn others — but we cannot confirm, investigate, or endorse any claim. Treat each report as a starting point for your own awareness, not as verified fact.

  • Reports are user-generated and not investigated by platform administrators.
  • Information should serve as one data point among many — always do your own research.
  • If you believe someone is in immediate danger, contact law enforcement, not just this site.

How to Use Browse

The browse directory contains community-submitted safety awareness reports. You can search by name or location, filter by country or US state, and explore an interactive map. Every report is user-submitted and unverified — use this as a starting point for awareness, never as a definitive verdict on anyone.

  • Search by name, city, state, or country using the search bar above.
  • Use the country/state dropdowns or the interactive maps to explore by location.
  • Click "Share" on any report to copy a direct link you can send to someone.

Before You Submit

Submitting a report adds a person's name, location, and the nature of your concern to a public directory. Please ensure your report is honest and submitted in good faith. You are responsible for what you submit. Reports are permanent but can be appealed by the subject via the platform's review process.

  • Submissions should reflect genuine safety concerns, not personal grievances.
  • Only letters (A–Z) are accepted for name and location to protect privacy.
  • You can submit up to 3 reports per hour from the same browser — no account needed.

Report Categories

Categories help others quickly understand the nature of the safety concern being reported. You may select multiple categories that apply to your experience. These are broad community classifications used for awareness — they are not legal definitions, and selecting them does not constitute a formal accusation.

  • Choose all categories that accurately reflect your experience — you can select more than one.
  • Categories appear as color-coded tags in the browse listing to help others filter by type.
  • If you're unsure which category fits, choose the closest one — accuracy helps the community.

Community Voices

Community Voices is a moderated, anonymous space for survivors, allies, and supporters. It is organized into rooms — each with its own purpose and norms — so you can find the conversation that fits where you are right now.

  • No names of specific people are permitted in any post or reply.
  • Posts and replies are reviewed before appearing — this usually takes 24–48 hours.
  • Your submission is completely anonymous — no personal data is collected or stored.
  • You can react to posts, save them to your device, and post replies — all without an account.
  • If you ever need to leave quickly, use the "Exit quickly" button at the top of this section.

How moderation works

All Community posts are reviewed by a volunteer moderator before appearing publicly. We remove posts that name specific individuals, contain unsafe content, or violate our community guidelines. Moderation is about safety, not censorship.

  • Posts are reviewed in order. Average wait time: 24–48 hours.
  • We remove: posts naming specific people, spam, and content that could put someone at risk.
  • We do not remove posts just because the topic is difficult or painful.
  • If your post is not approved, it is because it may have contained identifying information — you're welcome to revise and resubmit.
  • Community guidelines are publicly available in the open-source repository.

Your Privacy

SafeVoice is built from the ground up to protect your privacy. You never need to create an account, provide an email, or log in to use any feature. We have deliberately designed the system to minimize what we know about you — because your safety and anonymity are the entire point.

  • No accounts, no emails, no passwords — ever. Completely anonymous use.
  • We never store your IP address. A temporary check is used only for rate limiting and expires in 60 seconds.
  • The only browser data stored is a random anonymous ID in localStorage to enforce the 3-per-hour rate limit. You can clear it anytime by clearing your browser storage.
